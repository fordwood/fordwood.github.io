Wood for Fords / Fordwood

 

Model A Fordor Body Styles

The "60 A or B" is a Briggs built body. It has blind rear quarter panels and the top material covers the complete top & back, down to the belt rail moulding. Car was made in 1928 - 1929.

The 1929 "60 C" is a Briggs built body. It has blind rear quarter panels and the top material covers the complete top to the notched metal back. The back is steel and painted body color.

The 1929-1930-1931 "170 A or B" is a BRIGGS built body. It has a blind rear quarter panels and the top material covers only the center roof opening. Top sides and back of body are steel and painted the body color.
The 1929-1930-1931 "155 A-B-C-D" were built by Briggs and Murray. They have side windows in the rear quarter panels.Sometime people refer to these as a "3window" Fordor, because they are counting the front door, the rear door and the quarter window. The top material covers the center roof opening. The back and top sides are painted the body color.

In the 1931 Briggs and Murray both built the steel bodied "160". There is wood in the top and some tacking wood in the interior body panels.  The "160" series can be reconized by it's slanted windshield, a characteristic of some of the other 1931 bodies.
